The Earliest Spark, Engineered Right

Curiosity Infrastructure: Ages 0-6

A student's relationship with curiosity is set before kindergarten, which is why we design curriculum infrastructure specifically for the 0-6 age range rather than scaling down a K-12 product. Our SunnyHive program gives preschools and family childcare programs a turnkey or supplementary system for building curiosity-first habits from the earliest years.

SunnyHive can be the full solution for a group providing education and childcare for ages 0-6, or a supplementary digital tracking system layered onto an existing preschool. It draws on Montessori, Waldorf, and Reggio Emilia principles — not as a marketing label, but as the pedagogical basis for tools designed around a simple premise: a young child can teach themselves once the right tools and the right degree of guided freedom are put in front of them. Coaches and teachers are available to help students engage from anywhere, and DreamTrack begins building each child's evidence-of-growth record from this earliest stage of the Dream Quest journey.

Partnering with us gives an early-years program a curriculum foundation built for lifelong learning from day one. We push young students to engage safely, hands-on, and with real technology from the start, and we've consulted with early-years educators ranging from family childcare co-ops to large preschool networks, so we understand how to design within real budget constraints.

We also license SunnyHive to facilitators who complete our training program. If that's of interest, we suggest getting involved in our Ignite Curiosity community first to see whether our approach fits what you're building.

SunnyHive is the earliest stage of a longer arc, moved forward through the Curiosity Consortium and a set of interconnected learning programs. The Consortium carries a Dreamer from preschool through career advancement, built on the frontier disciplines the next century's workforce will need.

Consortium programs are organized by age and community need and connected by a common thread: the S.P.A.R.K. framework and the Dream Quest learning journey. Even at the preschool stage, we treat AI, computational thinking, and emerging-technology literacy as literacies worth introducing early — through play, not screens-first instruction — and DreamTrack begins each Dreamer's evidence-of-growth record from these first years. Curiosity Research Corporation continues to bring on early-years partners to refine this approach while staying true to a pedagogical method built from first principles: every young learner has the capacity to direct their own path when given the right tools.

Global Literacy

We start building fluency in AI, finance, and code well before a child reads fluently — the same way early language immersion works, applied to the literacies a lifetime will run on.

Frontier Disciplines

Even at the earliest ages, the foundation comes from practitioners in AI, physics, and aerospace — not a generic early-childhood STEM kit.

Family-Centered Design

Our early-years tools are designed to include parents and caregivers as active partners in a child's earliest learning habits.
