Dream Building as a Language

We treat dreaming as a research discipline — ideation, prototyping, and the disciplined process of failure that a lab runs before it finds what works.

Instar Lab is our research and development facility, where scientists, physicists, astronomers, and product engineers conceptualize and prototype solutions for problems civilization hasn't fully named yet. We teach dream literacy by letting students shadow that process directly, learning a repeatable method for turning a dream into a specification, and a specification into something real.

The Instar Lab

  • Next Century Civilization™
  • Sustainable Models

  • Aerospace & AI

We measure this work against the arc of civilization, not the school year — what physicists call moving up the Kardashev scale, toward a higher-energy, higher-capability future. Treating dreaming as a language lets us break that arc down into a model students can repeat: the same method our researchers use to move from open question to working prototype, instrumented through DreamTrack and the Capstone process so a student's trajectory stays auditable, not just aspirational.

In our Next Century Civilization™ (NCC) we focus on important aspects of literacy for the world our great-grandchildren will live in. We bring into fruition the dreams and designs to make life at that stage understandable today.

In Anthropological Linguistics we focus on language in its wider social and cultural context, and its role in making and maintaining cultural practices and societal structures in combination with NCCs.

We partner with innovative Aerospace & AI Enterprises to build policy, procedure, and to create languages around their dreams to allow future generations to digest a collective vision more easily.

Global Literacy

Curiosity Corporation designs literacy programs built for global reach, delivered in person through Curiosity Chapters and online through Spark Sessions and the Hive, and proven region by region through Curiosity Community, our flagship field test bed in the Mid-Ohio Valley.

Frontier Disciplines

We teach AI, physics, aerospace, and computational thinking as core literacies — designed by practitioners who work these disciplines daily, not curriculum vendors.
