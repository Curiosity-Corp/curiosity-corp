[PREAMBLE]
Curiosity Research Corporation is a credible-futurist education nonprofit — "Igniting Curiosity" into
frontier-tech pedagogy. Every image must look like a photo a serious research institute or aerospace
lab would publish in its own annual report: aspirational but documentary, not stock-photo sci-fi and
not concept art. Think "real lab, real students, slightly elevated lighting."

Subjects: diverse students (varied age, race, gender) genuinely engaged with frontier-tech tools — AI
interfaces, robotics, aerospace hardware, physics demonstration rigs, advanced computing setups,
data-visualization dashboards. Prefer object/environment/hands-only framing over posed portraits;
where a human presence is implied, show hands, wrists, backs, or silhouettes engaged with the
technology rather than posed faces-to-camera.

Settings: modern lab and maker-space environments, clean and well-equipped but not sterile;
occasional aerospace/physics-adjacent settings (observatories, fabrication spaces, mission-control-style
rooms, launch/rocketry hardware) for higher-aspiration pages (impact, mission, government/institutional
partner pages); outdoor settings only when paired with visible technology (a tablet, a drone, sensors)
to avoid generic "kids in a field" NGO imagery.

Palette: cool, clean, slightly high-contrast — blues, teals, and whites consistent with the site's
brand palette (colors/color1.css, primary teal #42b8d4-family), with warm amber/orange accent lighting
used sparingly to suggest "spark" or "ignition" moments (screen glow, a single warm practical light).

Lighting: directional and a little dramatic — single strong light sources, rim lighting, screen-glow
on surfaces and hands during tech interaction. Avoid flat, even, catalog-style lighting.

Level of futurism: near-future, plausible, photographic — not CGI, not abstract sci-fi concept art,
not over-stylized illustration. The credibility test: would a grant reviewer believe this photo
represents a real program?
